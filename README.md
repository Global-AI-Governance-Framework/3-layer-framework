# Understanding Global AI Governance Through a Three-Layer Framework

Welcome to the official repository for the **Three-Layer Framework for Global AI Governance**. 

This project proposes a multi-layered framework to conceptualize and navigate the complex, fragmented landscape of global AI policy. Inspired by the classic 3-layer model of internet governance, we adapt this structure to address the unique challenges of the AI ecosystem.

The rapid growth of AI policy initiatives (such as the Pax Silica Initiative and the Agentic AI Foundation) has created a risk of duplication, overlap, and interoperability challenges. Our framework aims to:

* **Provide a common taxonomy** for policymakers and researchers.
* **Identify key actors** and international bodies at each level of the AI stack.
* **Enable targeted policy solutions** that anticipate spillover effects across layers.

## ðŸ—ï¸ The Three Layers
Our framework categorizes AI activity into three distinct layers:

1. **The Infrastructure Layer:** The physical foundation, including semiconductors (GPUs, TPUs), data centers, cloud platforms, and energy systems.
2. **The Logical Layer:** The software, proprietary and open-source foundation models, frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow), and interoperability protocols (e.g., MCP).
3. **The Social Layer:** Human and institutional interactions where AI applications (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) are used for content creation, B2B, and G2C services.

> **Note:** Ethical considerations are treated as a cross-cutting topic that permeates every layer, rather than a separate category.
