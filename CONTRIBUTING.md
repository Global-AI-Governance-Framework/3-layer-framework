# Contributing to the Three-Layer AI Governance Framework

First off, thank you for considering contributing to this framework! It is through community feedback and diverse perspectives that we can build a more robust model for global AI governance.

We welcome contributions from researchers, policymakers, developers, and AI ethicists.

## üåü How You Can Help

You can contribute in several ways:

1.  **Refining the Layers:** Suggest improvements to the definitions of the Infrastructure, Logical, and Social layers.
2.  **Mapping Actors:** Help us identify and place international bodies, NGOs, or regulatory acts within the correct layers.
3.  **Proposing Verticals:** Identify emerging trends (like Autonomous Agents) that span across multiple layers.
4.  **Case Studies:** Provide real-world examples of how this framework can be applied to specific geopolitical or technical challenges.

### 1. Opening an Issue
Before making a major change, we recommend [opening an issue](https://github.com/Global-AI-Governance-Framework/3-layer-framework/issues) to discuss your proposal. This allows for:
* Clarification of the idea.
* Coordination with the authors (Cedric & Moshe).
* Feedback from other community members.

### 2. Submitting a Pull Request (PR)
If you have a specific edit to the text or the framework structure:
1. **Fork** the repository.
2. Create a new **branch** for your suggestion.

## üìú Ethical Guidelines
As mentioned in the framework, ethical considerations are cross-cutting. We expect all contributions to:
* Be evidence-based and professionally reasoned.
* Respect diverse cultural and geopolitical perspectives.
* Maintain a constructive and collaborative tone.

By submitting a contribution, you agree that your work will be licensed under the same license as this repository.
